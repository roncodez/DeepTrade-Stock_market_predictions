Lesson 1: PE/Ratio(TTM)
The PE Ratio, or Price-to-Earnings Ratio, is a measure that compares the price of a company's stock to its earnings per share (EPS). Essentially, it tells you how much investors are willing to pay for each rupees of a company's earnings. The "TTM" stands for "Trailing Twelve Months," indicating that the ratio is calculated using the earnings over the past year.

Lesson 2:Industry P/E
Let's say you and some other kids in your neighborhood all have lemonade stands. Each stand makes different amounts of money, and people pay different amounts to own a piece of those stands (like we talked about with PE Ratio). Now, imagine you want to figure out how much people usually pay to own a piece of any lemonade stand in your neighborhood, not just yours. So, you look at all the lemonade stands, see how much money they made, and how much people paid for shares in each one. The "Industry PE" is like an average of all these ratios from all the lemonade stands in the neighborhood. It shows you, on average, how much people are willing to pay for a share compared to how much money the stands make. So if most people are paying a lot more for each lemonade stand share than what the stands earn, the Industry PE will be high. This means that people really think lemonade stands in your neighborhood are super cool and will make more money later on. If the number is low, it means people aren't paying much more than what the stands are earning right now.
